Playing Cards as Tarot!

Learn how to use a regular deck of playing cards for magicks!, March 11th, 2023

Playing Cards can easily be used as Tarot cards through practice and dedication.

How to start?

As a person belief I believe that playing cards hold no magic unless WE give it to them. It's why I personally recommend cleansing your cards, and shuffling them, and getting acquainted with them through hand movements and basic spreads

How to do a reading?

Yes or No Readings

Clear your mind! and focus innately on your yes-or-no question. Breathe deeply, then breathe out. Next draw a card. You should usually decide this prior, but red means yes and black means no. The cards even have their own meaning to give details on the yes or no question(see below)

Card Meanings

To begin with, one of the big things is deciding the suit the card represents for yourself or querent.
  • Hearts: Like the cups suit in the tarot, hearts represent your feelings, relationships, and home.
  • Spades: Like the swords in the tarot, spades connect to obstacles, loss, planning, and intellect.
  • Clubs: Like wands in the tarot, clubs represent future actions, travel, and creative thinking.
  • Diamonds: Like pentacles in the tarot, diamonds connect to work and money.


Next up, we'll decide what the specific numbers and cards each mean. TRHe following list below will give you a good basis

  • Ace: Beginning, attributes of the card suit
  • 2: Relationship, balance
  • 3: Connections, growth
  • 4: Foundation, stability
  • 5: Change, challenge, imbalance
  • 6: Harmony, success
  • 7: Wisdom, spirituality, choices
  • 8: Organization, movement, stagnation
  • 9: Realization, understanding
  • 10: Completion, transition to something new

How to give more indepth readings?

Since using these playing cards is entirely based on intuition, assigning them your **OWN** special meaning can help you get more accurate and correct readings.
